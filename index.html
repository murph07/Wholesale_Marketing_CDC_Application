The landscape you play in

The market is crowded.
The opportunity is real.

Café du Cycliste occupies a specific position — premium, craft, values-led — that most competitors either can't reach or can't sustain. Here's what that means in practice.

Rapha

Strong wholesale presence, but perceived as increasingly corporate after LVMH involvement. Café du Cycliste's independence and community identity — the Nice roots, the Outsiders ethos — is a real differentiator for wholesale partners who want to carry something with genuine character.

Opportunity

Where the gap sits

Most premium cycling brands ship product and PDFs. The brands that win at wholesale build co-marketing programs around events, seasonal content, and community. CdC already has Caravan, The Grip, and the Outsiders Club. That infrastructure can be packaged for wholesale partners to use.

Three moves I'd explore in the first 90 days

Where I'd start.
First 90 days.

These can be starting points. The actual priorities would come from listening to the team and partners first.

01
Map the partner activation gap
Insight: Partners who receive both product and activation support sell through at higher rates than those who receive product alone.
Before proposing anything, I'd audit what wholesale partners currently receive — asset quality, seasonal calendars, co-marketing support — and where the gaps are compared to what competitors offer. A clear baseline makes future investment decisions easier to justify.
Output: Activation audit within 30 days
02
Build seasonal content kits per collection
Insight: The six collection identities are strong but underused in wholesale. Partners default to generic brand imagery because it's the easiest thing to deploy.
Working with the Brand & Activation Manager, I'd develop modular toolkits for each collection — POS layouts, social templates, in-store guides — that partners can localise without losing the brand voice. Ready-to-deploy, not request-based.
Output: First toolkit live for SS26 season
03
Connect community assets to wholesale activation
Insight: Café du Cycliste has an active riding community — Outsiders Club, Caravan, The Grip — that generates real content. Wholesale partners rarely get to use any of it.
I'd explore a lightweight program where key wholesale partners, particularly specialist bike shops, can co-brand with local CdC ride events or community content. It gives partners a reason to feature the brand year-round, not just at product launch.
Output: Pilot with 2–3 key partners, Q3
